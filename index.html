Why people switch

A chess tool,
not a chess platform.

Most chess sites want your email, your username, your rating, your attention and ten minutes of your life before they'll tell you whether Bxc6 was a blunder. ChessMate doesn't.

🔒

Your games stay on your phone

No sign-up, no CAPTCHA, no email verification. Your library lives in your device's local storage — it doesn't sync, doesn't get sold, doesn't get breached. Delete the app and it's gone.

🎯

Real analysis, not a toy

The engine Lichess trusts — Stockfish 17 — running inside your browser, with the same Brilliant-to-Blunder classification rules chess.com uses, applied by a battle-tested open-source reporter.

📱

Built for one hand and a thumb

A native-first layout: fixed bottom-nav, a glass-blur control bar, a drag-to-jump eval graph, tap-to-move dots, swipe navigation, autoplay, and a Replay button that just works.

⚡

Fast enough to feel instant

Lichess's free cloud eval is tried first — depth-30+ in well under a second. For anything else the bundled Stockfish worker takes over at the depth you choose, and cancels cleanly on any tap.

The classification colours

Ten verdicts,
one honest scale.

Every move earns exactly one badge — on the list, floating over the destination square, and as a dot on the graph. From a tactic only a search would find, to the move that cost you the game.

How it feels to use it

Open. Paste. Find your blunders. Close.

That's the whole pitch. Nothing happens to your data. Nothing is sent to anyone.

01

Paste a PGN

The home screen shows a tiny board with the final position and a tap-to-load button. The board flies in.

02

Tap Analyse

A tournament-gold button starts Stockfish. A progress bar fills as evaluations stream in.

03

Read the report

White & black percentages, a per-badge breakdown card, and an SVG curve with teal brilliants and red blunders.

04

Replay & explore

Swipe the moves with soft sounds, tap a suggestion to branch a variation, drag the graph to scrub.
